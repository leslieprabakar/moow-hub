Moow.Hub® — Legal Framework

Partnership Agreement

Terms and conditions governing the partnership between Moow.Hub® and its partners worldwide.

Purpose

This website and mobile responsive design will promote yoga, wellness, preventive healthcare, educational initiatives, fundraising, corporate wellness, schools, NGOs, charities, hospitals, Rotary, Lions Clubs, distributors, retailers, government bodies, and community organisations.

Definitions

'Moow.Hub®' includes the website, mobile application, QR-code ecosystem, educational content, digital services and future enhancements.

'Products' include T-Shirts, printed materials and associated merchandise.

Scope

Partner may market, distribute, promote and conduct wellness programmes using approved Moow.Hub® products and branding.

Company Obligations

  • Supply products
  • Maintain digital platform
  • Provide approved branding assets
  • Provide training material
  • Provide reasonable partner support

Partner Obligations

  • Promote ethically
  • Comply with laws
  • Protect brand reputation
  • Avoid misleading claims
  • Maintain records
  • Pay invoices
  • Provide campaign metrics upon request

Intellectual Property

All copyrights, trademarks, patents, software, artwork, illustrations, QR codes, databases, educational content, logos and know-how remain exclusively owned by Moow.Hub®. No ownership transfers under this Agreement.

Brand Usage

Brand assets may only be used according to the Brand Guidelines. Any co-branding requires prior written approval.

Orders & Payments

Commercial terms shall be defined in purchase orders. Default terms: 50% advance and balance before dispatch unless otherwise agreed.

Shipping

Shipping, insurance, import duties, customs clearance and local taxes are allocated in the applicable purchase order or Incoterms.

Confidentiality

Each Party shall keep confidential all business, pricing, customer, technical and strategic information. This survives five years after termination or longer where required by law.

Data Protection

Each Party shall independently comply with applicable privacy legislation, including where applicable India's Digital Personal Data Protection Act, UK GDPR, EU GDPR or equivalent local laws.

Compliance

Each Party shall comply with anti-bribery, anti-corruption, sanctions, export control and applicable regulatory requirements.

Warranty

Products will substantially conform to agreed specifications. Except where prohibited by law, all other warranties are excluded.

Limitation of Liability

Neither Party shall be liable for indirect or consequential damages. Aggregate liability shall not exceed the value of the affected order except where local law prohibits such limitation.

Force Majeure

Neither Party is liable for delays caused by events beyond reasonable control.

Term

Initial term of three years with automatic one-year renewals unless notice is given.

Termination

  • 30 days written notice without cause
  • Immediate termination for material breach
  • Immediate termination for insolvency
  • Immediate termination for illegal conduct
  • Immediate termination for misuse of intellectual property

Post-Termination

Partner shall cease using Moow.Hub® IP, return confidential information and settle outstanding payments.

Governing Law

The Parties shall select the applicable governing law by completing appropriate Schedule (Schedule A) before execution. Suggested options include India, England & Wales, EU member state or another mutually agreed jurisdiction.

Dispute Resolution

Negotiation followed by mediation where practical and then arbitration or competent courts as selected in Schedule (Schedule A).

Entire Agreement

This Agreement together with its schedules constitutes the entire agreement.

Schedules

The following Schedules form an integral part of this Agreement. Each Schedule is incorporated by reference and shall govern in the event of any conflict with the main body of this Agreement.

  • Schedule A: Country Legal Addendum — Governing law, jurisdiction, and regulatory compliance
  • Schedule B: Pricing — Product pricing, payment terms, and financial obligations
  • Schedule C: Brand Guidelines — Logo usage, branding standards, and approval process
  • Schedule D: Data Processing Addendum — Data protection, privacy compliance, and processing terms
  • Schedule E: Product Catalogue — Product descriptions, specifications, and quality standards
  • Schedule F: Statement of Work / Purchase Orders — SOW templates, delivery, and acceptance criteria

Partner Access Required

Full schedule details including pricing, brand guidelines, and commercial terms are available exclusively to Moow.Hub® partners.

Schedule A — Country Legal Addendum

This Addendum supplements the main Agreement with jurisdiction-specific provisions applicable to the Partner's country of operation. Parties must complete Section 1 prior to execution.

A.1 — Governing Law Selection

The Parties shall select one of the following jurisdictions as the governing law for this Agreement by completing the relevant field below:

  • Republic of India
  • England and Wales
  • A member state of the European Union
  • United States of America (State of __________)
  • Other mutually agreed jurisdiction: __________

A.2 — Jurisdiction & Venue

Subject to the dispute resolution clause in the main Agreement, the courts of the selected jurisdiction shall have exclusive jurisdiction over any proceedings arising out of or in connection with this Agreement.

A.3 — Regulatory Compliance

Each Party shall comply with all applicable laws, regulations, ordinances, and statutes in its respective jurisdiction, including but not limited to:

  • Consumer protection laws
  • Product safety and labelling standards
  • Import/export regulations and customs requirements
  • Tax obligations including GST, VAT, sales tax or equivalent
  • Foreign exchange control regulations
  • Anti-money laundering (AML) requirements
  • Sanctions and export control regulations

A.4 — Country-Specific Provisions

India: This Agreement shall be subject to the Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996 (as amended). GST and applicable Indian taxes shall be borne as per the invoice. Foreign exchange transactions shall comply with RBI regulations.

United Kingdom: This Agreement shall be subject to the laws of England and Wales. UK GDPR and the Data Protection Act 2018 shall apply to any personal data processing.

European Union: This Agreement shall be subject to the laws of the selected EU member state. EU GDPR shall apply. The Parties shall comply with applicable consumer rights directives.

United States: This Agreement shall be subject to the laws of the selected state. The Parties shall comply with applicable federal and state consumer protection laws, including the FTC Act where applicable.

A.5 — Language

This Agreement is executed in English. In the event of any translation, the English version shall prevail except where local law requires a mandatory local-language version.

Schedule C — Brand Guidelines

This Schedule governs the Partner's use of Moow.Hub® brand assets, including logos, trademarks, colour palettes, and co-branding requirements.

C.1 — Approved Brand Assets

The following brand assets are available for Partner use upon written approval:

  • Moow.Hub® primary logo (full colour, monochrome, and reversed)
  • Moow.Hub® wordmark
  • Moow.Hub® icon / favicon
  • Approved colour palette (see Section C.3)
  • Approved typography (see Section C.4)
  • QR code templates (pre-approved designs only)
  • Marketing collateral templates (as provided by Moow.Hub®)

C.2 — Logo Usage Rules

  • Minimum clear space: Height of the "M" in the Moow.Hub® logo on all sides
  • Minimum size: 24px height for digital; 10mm height for print
  • The logo must not be stretched, distorted, rotated, or altered in any way
  • The logo must only be placed on backgrounds that provide sufficient contrast
  • The logo must not be used as a pattern or decorative element
  • The logo must not be used in proximity to any other logos without prior written approval
  • All approved assets must be downloaded from the official Moow.Hub® Partner Portal

C.3 — Colour Palette

  • Primary: Terracotta — #D4735E (RGB: 212, 115, 94)
  • Secondary: Gold — #C9A96E (RGB: 201, 169, 110)
  • Dark: Navy — #1A2332 (RGB: 26, 35, 50)
  • Light: Warm White — #FAF7F3 (RGB: 250, 247, 243)
  • Colour combinations must maintain WCAG 2.1 AA contrast standards for accessibility

C.4 — Typography

  • Headings: Playfair Display (Serif)
  • Body Text: Inter (Sans-Serif)
  • Fonts must be licensed appropriately for the intended use
  • Font modifications (e.g., outlined, shadowed) are not permitted without approval

C.5 — Co-Branding

Any co-branded materials featuring both the Moow.Hub® brand and the Partner's brand (or any third-party brand) require prior written approval from Moow.Hub®. The co-branding request must include:

  • Detailed mock-up of the proposed co-branded material
  • Intended use and distribution channels
  • Duration of use
  • Territory of distribution

C.6 — Prohibited Uses

The Partner shall NOT:

  • Use the Moow.Hub® brand in any manner that could be considered defamatory, misleading, or harmful to reputation
  • Register or attempt to register any Moow.Hub® trademarks, domain names, or social media handles
  • Use the Moow.Hub® brand in connection with any illegal, harmful, or controversial products or activities
  • Alter, modify, or create derivative versions of any Moow.Hub® brand assets
  • Use the Moow.Hub® brand after termination of this Agreement

C.7 — Approval Process

All uses of Moow.Hub® brand assets outside of pre-approved templates must be submitted to brand@moow-hub.com for review. Moow.Hub® shall respond within 5 business days. No use shall commence until written approval is received.

C.8 — Brand Audit

Moow.Hub® reserves the right to conduct periodic brand audits to ensure compliance with this Schedule. The Partner shall cooperate fully and provide access to all materials featuring Moow.Hub® branding within 5 business days of request.

Schedule D — Data Processing Addendum

This Addendum sets out the terms under which personal data is processed in connection with this Agreement. It applies where either Party processes personal data on behalf of the other.

D.1 — Definitions

  • Data Controller: The Party that determines the purposes and means of processing personal data
  • Data Processor: The Party that processes personal data on behalf of the Data Controller
  • Data Subject: An identified or identifiable natural person whose personal data is processed
  • Personal Data: Any information relating to an identified or identifiable natural person
  • Processing: Any operation performed on personal data, including collection, recording, storage, adaptation, retrieval, consultation, use, disclosure, erasure, or destruction

D.2 — Roles & Responsibilities

Each Party's role shall depend on the context of processing:

  • Customer Data: Moow.Hub® acts as Data Processor; Partner acts as Data Controller
  • Platform Analytics: Moow.Hub® acts as Data Controller
  • Marketing Data: The Party initiating the marketing campaign acts as Data Controller

D.3 — Types of Data Processed

  • Partner contact details (name, email, phone, address)
  • End-customer information (name, email, delivery address) — only for fulfilment purposes
  • Transaction data (order history, payment references — no card details stored)
  • Platform usage analytics (page views, session data, device information)
  • Marketing preferences and consent records

D.4 — Processing Purposes

  • Order fulfilment and product delivery
  • Account management and partner support
  • Platform improvement and analytics
  • Marketing communications (with explicit consent)
  • Legal and regulatory compliance
  • Fraud prevention and security

D.5 — Data Security Measures

Each Party shall implement appropriate technical and organisational measures, including:

  • Encryption of personal data in transit and at rest (AES-256 or equivalent)
  • Access controls and authentication mechanisms
  • Regular security assessments and vulnerability testing
  • Employee training on data protection
  • Incident response procedures
  • Physical security of premises where data is processed

D.6 — Data Subject Rights

Each Party shall assist the other in responding to data subject requests within 30 days, including:

  • Right of access (Subject Access Request)
  • Right to rectification
  • Right to erasure ("right to be forgotten")
  • Right to restrict processing
  • Right to data portability
  • Right to object to processing
  • Right not to be subject to automated decision-making

D.7 — Sub-Processors

The Data Processor shall not engage sub-processors without prior written consent of the Data Controller. Where sub-processors are approved, the Data Processor shall ensure equivalent data protection obligations through contractual arrangements.

D.8 — Cross-Border Transfers

Personal data shall not be transferred outside the jurisdiction of collection without appropriate safeguards, including:

  • Adequacy decisions (where applicable)
  • Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs)
  • Binding Corporate Rules (BCRs)
  • Explicit consent of the Data Subject

D.9 — Data Breach Notification

In the event of a personal data breach, the affected Party shall notify the other Party within 72 hours of becoming aware of the breach, providing:

  • Nature of the breach and categories/number of Data Subjects affected
  • Name and contact details of the data protection officer
  • Likely consequences of the breach
  • Measures taken or proposed to address the breach

D.10 — Data Retention

Personal data shall be retained only for as long as necessary for the purposes for which it was collected, or as required by applicable law. Upon termination of this Agreement, the Data Processor shall, at the Data Controller's choice, return or delete all personal data within 30 days.

D.11 — Applicable Law

This Addendum shall be governed by the data protection laws applicable in the jurisdiction selected in Schedule A, including but not limited to:

  • India's Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023 (DPDPA)
  • UK General Data Protection Regulation (UK GDPR)
  • EU General Data Protection Regulation (EU GDPR)
  • California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA) / California Privacy Rights Act (CPRA)
  • Other applicable local data protection legislation

Schedule F — Statement of Work / Purchase Orders

This Schedule governs the process for issuing Statements of Work (SOWs) and Purchase Orders (POs) under this Agreement, including delivery schedules, acceptance criteria, and change management.

F.1 — Statement of Work (SOW) Process

For each project or engagement, the Parties shall agree a Statement of Work containing:

  • Project scope and objectives
  • Deliverables and milestones
  • Timeline and deadlines
  • Pricing and payment schedule (referencing Schedule B)
  • Roles and responsibilities of each Party
  • Acceptance criteria
  • Any jurisdiction-specific requirements (referencing Schedule A)

F.2 — Purchase Order Process

  • Step 1: Partner submits PO to Moow.Hub® including: product SKUs, quantities, delivery address, requested delivery date, and any special instructions
  • Step 2: Moow.Hub® confirms or amends the PO within 3 business days
  • Step 3: Upon confirmation, Partner remits advance payment per Schedule B
  • Step 4: Moow.Hub® dispatches order and provides tracking details
  • Step 5: Partner confirms receipt and inspects goods

F.4 — Delivery Terms

  • Standard Delivery: 15–21 business days from order confirmation (subject to stock availability)
  • Express Delivery: 5–10 business days (additional charges may apply)
  • International Shipping: 20–30 business days depending on destination and customs processing
  • Moow.Hub® shall provide tracking information within 2 business days of dispatch
  • Risk of loss passes to the Partner upon delivery to the designated carrier (Incoterms® 2020: FCA Moow.Hub® Warehouse)

F.5 — Acceptance Criteria

The Partner shall inspect all delivered goods within 7 business days of receipt. Goods shall be deemed accepted if no written rejection is received within this period. Rejection must include:

  • PO number and SKU details
  • Photographs of defective or incorrect items
  • Description of the defect or discrepancy
  • Quantity affected

F.6 — Returns & Replacements

  • Defective Goods: Moow.Hub® shall replace or refund defective products at no additional cost to the Partner
  • Incorrect Dispatch: Moow.Hub® shall arrange return and re-dispatch at no additional cost
  • Change of Mind: Returns for change of mind are not accepted once goods have been dispatched
  • Return Window: All returns must be initiated within 14 days of delivery

F.7 — Change Management

Any changes to a confirmed PO must be submitted in writing. Moow.Hub® shall assess the impact of requested changes and respond within 3 business days. Changes may result in adjusted pricing and/or delivery timelines.

F.8 — Acceptance & Sign-Off

Upon satisfactory delivery and inspection, the Partner shall confirm acceptance in writing (email confirmation is acceptable). Moow.Hub® shall issue a Delivery Confirmation and final invoice upon acceptance.
